About Us

The Competition

Bhangra in the Burgh brings together the top bhangra teams from across the nation to compete on one stage in a night of music, dance, and South Asian culture. The competition, which is the largest student run event in Pittsburgh, has attracted a sold out crowd of 2,500 people for the past seven years. The Bhangra in the Burgh organization uses the competition as a vehicle to raise money every year for a local charity. From the time that this organization was founded, we have raised over $75,000 and hope to go to unprecedented heights this year.

The Dance

Bhangra is a lively and energetic folk dance that is usually performed in celebration of the annual harvest. It originates from a region of extending from Northwest India to East Pakistan, known as Punjab. The dance is accompanied with a combination of various classical instruments, such as the ‘dhol’, along with traditional lyrics. By fusing with Western elements, such as hip-hop and reggae, bhangra has been dramatically transformed, capturing the attention of young adults throughout the United States and Canada.

The Charity

This year we are proud to support Pittsburgh Promise. This local organization aims to promote academic success and to encourage students have aspirations and goals. They reform struggling urban schools, provide scholarships for underprivelaged children, and provide a network of support to anyone in need of guidance. We can't wait to assist them in their mission!
